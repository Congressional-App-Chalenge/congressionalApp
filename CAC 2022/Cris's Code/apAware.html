Are AP's Even Worth It?

An AP score of a 3 is guaranteed to get students credit at about 58% of public and 33% of private colleges. Getting credit lets you be exempt from taking the classes in college for which you took the AP tests, which means you pay less because you are taking fewer classes. This means taking AP exams can cut down the amount of time you have to spend in college, greatly decreasing the cost you have to spend on college. 1 college credit hour means an average of about $621 at 4-year institutions or $303 at 2-year institutions. The only question is would you like to spend a little time either self-studying for the test or taking the class in highschool and pay around $100 now or have crippling college debt later.

According to research done by the College Board, students scoring a 2 or higher on the tests are more likely to do very well in college introductory classes as AP testing and preparation provides a window for students to look into the college experience.
